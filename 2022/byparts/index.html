Review of the Basic Theorems

In what follows, UUU is assumed to be an open, bounded subset of Rn\mathbb R ^nRn with C1C^1C1 boundary and uuu, vvv sufficiently regular functions. Then

∫Uuxidx=∫∂UuνidS \int_U u_{x_i} dx = \int_{\partial U} u\nu_i dS ∫U​uxi​​dx=∫∂U​uνi​dS

is the basic identity from which the following are built. Applied to the product uvuvuv, this gives

∫Uuxivdx=−∫Uuvxidx+∫∂UuvνidS \int_U u_{x_i}v dx = -\int_U uv_{x_i} dx+\int_{\partial U} uv\nu_i dS ∫U​uxi​​vdx=−∫U​uvxi​​dx+∫∂U​uvνi​dS

And applying (1) to the first-order derivatives uxiu_{x_i}uxi​​ and adding up the equalities we obtain

∫UΔudx=∫∂U∂u∂νdS \int_U \Delta u dx = \int_{\partial U} \frac{\partial u}{\partial \nu} dS ∫U​Δudx=∫∂U​∂ν∂u​dS

where ∂u∂ν\frac{\partial u}{\partial \nu}∂ν∂u​ is defined to be equal to the inner product between the gradient and the outward unit vector, ⟨gradu,ν⟩\langle \textnormal{grad} u , \nu \rangle⟨gradu,ν⟩. Analogously, applying again (1) to the products uvxiu v_{x_i}uvxi​​ and summing over iii one gets

∫U⟨gradu,gradv⟩dx=−∫∂UuΔvdx+∫∂Uu∂v∂νdS \int_U \langle \textnormal{grad} u, \textnormal{grad} v \rangle dx = -\int_{\partial U} u\Delta v dx +\int_{\partial U} u \frac{\partial v}{\partial \nu} dS ∫U​⟨gradu,gradv⟩dx=−∫∂U​uΔvdx+∫∂U​u∂ν∂v​dS

Finally, taking advantage of symmetry in the LHS of (4) by expressing it in terms of Δu\Delta uΔu results in

∫U(uΔv−vΔu)dx=∫∂U(u∂v∂ν−v∂u∂ν)dS \int_U (u\Delta v-v\Delta u) dx = \int_{\partial U} \left ( u\frac{\partial v}{\partial \nu}-v\frac{\partial u}{\partial \nu} \right ) dS ∫U​(uΔv−vΔu)dx=∫∂U​(u∂ν∂v​−v∂ν∂u​)dS

Two often overlooked identities are related to the biharmonic operator and fall as corollaries of the former relations are

∫UuΔ2vdx=∫∂Uu∂Δv∂νdS−∫∂UΔv∂u∂νdS+∫UΔuΔvdx \int_U u \Delta^2 v dx= \int_{\partial U} u \frac{ \partial \Delta v}{\partial \nu} dS -\int_{\partial U} \Delta v \frac{\partial u}{\partial \nu} dS +\int_U \Delta u \Delta v dx ∫U​uΔ2vdx=∫∂U​u∂ν∂Δv​dS−∫∂U​Δv∂ν∂u​dS+∫U​ΔuΔvdx

and for the symmetrical case

∫U(uΔ2v−vΔ2u)dx=∫∂U(u∂Δv∂ν−v∂Δu∂ν)dS+∫∂U(Δu∂v∂ν−v∂u∂ν)dS \int_U \left ( u \Delta^2 v-v\Delta^2 u \right )dx= \int_{\partial U} \left ( u \frac{\partial \Delta v}{\partial \nu}- v\frac{\partial \Delta u}{\partial \nu} \right ) dS +\int_{\partial U} \left ( \Delta u \frac{\partial v}{\partial \nu}- v\frac{\partial u}{\partial \nu} \right )dS ∫U​(uΔ2v−vΔ2u)dx=∫∂U​(u∂ν∂Δv​−v∂ν∂Δu​)dS+∫∂U​(Δu∂ν∂v​−v∂ν∂u​)dS

as an evident consequence of (6). To prove (6), use (4) to see that

∫UuΔ2vdx=−∫U⟨gradu,gradΔv⟩dx+∫∂Uu∂Δv∂νdS \int_U u \Delta^2 v dx = -\int_U \langle \textnormal{grad} u, \textnormal{grad} \Delta v \rangle dx +\int_{\partial U} u \frac{\partial \Delta v}{\partial \nu} dS ∫U​uΔ2vdx=−∫U​⟨gradu,gradΔv⟩dx+∫∂U​u∂ν∂Δv​dS

and that the integral over UUU can in turn be decomposed as

∫U⟨gradu,gradΔv⟩dx=−∫UΔuΔvdx+∫∂UΔv∂u∂νdS \int_U \langle \textnormal{grad} u, \textnormal{grad}\Delta v \rangle dx= -\int_U \Delta u \Delta v dx + \int_{\partial U} \Delta v \frac{\partial u}{\partial \nu} dS ∫U​⟨gradu,gradΔv⟩dx=−∫U​ΔuΔvdx+∫∂U​Δv∂ν∂u​dS
